Summary

Summing it up, both cultural and economic factors have influence on the life expectancy.

First of all, the 6th chart is very illustrative in terms of the impact of education on the lifespan: the more the person is educated, the longer he or she is expected to live.

Secondly, from the 4th and the 5th graphs, it is clear, that economic situation in a state is also important in terms of life expectancy. The more developed the economy, the higher the life standarts are, and, as a result, the social environment is more beneficial for human health.

Overall, the average lifespan of a humankind flactuates around 65 years (from chart 7), which is rather deplorable. At the same time, graph 1 shows that quite many countries tend to have life expectancy rate at around 73 years. These results might appear strange, because from the first sight they show different figures for quite similar questions, but this phenomena can be explained: the countries with higher life expectancy generally tend to have less population.
